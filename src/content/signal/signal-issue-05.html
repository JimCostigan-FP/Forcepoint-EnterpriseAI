Fourteen teams. Four patterns. The company shows us what to build.

Issue #5 · Week ending June 14, 2026
Round 1 of the Forcepoint Enterprise AI Hackathon wrapped this week. Fourteen team submissions clustered into four architectural patterns, five projects move to fast-track architecture review, and a Forcepoint-branded presentation skill built outside the Enterprise AI team is ready for limited beta. The strongest signal of the week: the company is telling us what to build next — in working code.
This week at Forcepoint
AI Council reviewing the Enterprise AI Standard
The Council is now reviewing the Enterprise AI Standard — the document that establishes the requirements, guardrails, and responsibilities governing the development, deployment, and ongoing operation of AI systems at Forcepoint. We look forward to their feedback and approval.
Source: Jira · Target Architecture workstream
Hackathon analysis complete
All 14 submissions assessed against impact, execution, storytelling, and scalability, and clustered into four architectural patterns — connector skills, multi-source correlation, automation with escalation, and content generation. Five projects are flagged for fast-track architecture review.
Source: Jira · Hackathon workstream
Intake refinement underway
Hackathon findings are being folded into a revised use-case intake form, with explicit questions on data classification, orchestration approach, and token budgeting. Submitting an idea will be faster and clearer on Round 2.
Source: Jira · EAI Portal workstream
FIP portal continues to harden
The Forcepoint Intelligence Platform (FIP) is in steady iteration following last cycle's POC: end-to-end skill submission, security and functionality review, and publishing back to your organization's skills library remain the day-to-day path.
Source: Jira · EAI Portal workstream
From across the program
PowerPoint branding skill ready for beta
A working session this week confirmed the skill produces ~97% correct output when run directly inside the PowerPoint add-in with the Forcepoint template loaded. The build is now ready for limited internal beta with a small creative team.
Source: Branded Presentation Cohort Project
Visual ground truth, not just rules
Skill v0.3.0 was packaged with five reference slide images injected into every session — the skill compares its output against the canonical Forcepoint template, not just rules described in text. An 8-slide branded deck was generated end-to-end as proof.
Source: Branded Presentation Cohort Project
Five projects on the fast track
FOSS Reviewer, Product Maturity Assessment, Forcepoint TS Intelligence Console, DIANA, and Trinity Agent showed strong architectural coherence in Round 1 and are headed into per-system review and skill registration.
Source: Hackathon Project
